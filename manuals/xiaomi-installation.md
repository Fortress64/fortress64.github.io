# First-Time Flashing Instructions

## Step 1 — Back Up Your Data

All data on the device — including apps, settings, photos, and files — will be permanently deleted during the flashing process. There is no way to recover this data afterwards. Back up everything you wish to keep before proceeding.

---

## Step 2 — Flash Stock Firmware

Flashing the latest official firmware before installing a custom ROM ensures the baseband, modem, and vendor partitions are current and consistent.

Download the latest **and** last updated stock firmware for your device. Prefer the **Global** firmware if available for your device, otherwise select the firmware for your region.

## Step 3 — Install ADB and Fastboot

ADB and Fastboot are required to flash and communicate with your device from a PC.

> **Alternative:** If you do not prefer or cannot install ADB and Fastboot on your system, you can boot from our [Android Live Recovery USB image](https://fortress64.github.io/downloads/android-live-recovery-usb/), which includes all required tools out of the box.

---

## Step 4 — Enter Fastboot Mode

1. Power off the device completely.
2. Hold **Volume Down + Power** simultaneously until the Fastboot screen appears.

---

## Step 5 — Connect and Flash Stock Firmware

1. Connect the device to a PC via USB-C.
2. Open a terminal or command prompt.
3. Navigate to the extracted firmware directory and run the appropriate script:
   - **Windows:** `flash_all.bat`
   - **Linux:** `flash_all.sh`

---

## Step 6 — Verify the Flash and Complete MIUI Setup

- Confirm that all output lines end with a `SUCCESS` message, nothing may have `FAILED`.
- If the process fails, retry Step 5 or else force-reboot by holding the **Power Button + Volume Down** and try again if the phone buzzes and the Fastboot screen reappears.
- Wait for the MIUI setup wizard to appear, then complete the initial setup through to the home screen. This finalises the vendor post-installation process and is required before proceeding.

---

## Step 7 — Flash the Custom ROM

1. Download and extract your preferred ROM from your device's page, along with its accompanying `boot.img`, `vendor_boot.img`, and `init_boot.img` files (if they exist) before proceeding.
2. Re-enter Fastboot mode (see Step 4).
3. Open a terminal and run the following commands in sequence:

> Flash `boot.img` if it is present for your device. Confirm that the output ends with a `SUCCESS` message, and that it does not contain `FAILED`.

```bash
fastboot flash boot boot.img
```

> Flash `vendor_boot.img` if it is present for your device. Confirm that the output ends with a `SUCCESS` message, and that it does not contain `FAILED`.

```bash
fastboot flash vendor_boot vendor_boot.img
```

> Flash `init_boot.img` if it is present for your device. Confirm that the output ends with a `SUCCESS` message, and that it does not contain `FAILED`.

```bash
fastboot flash init_boot init_boot.img
```

```bash
fastboot reboot recovery
```

4. Once in recovery mode, navigate using the **Volume** buttons and confirm with **Power**.
5. Select **Apply Update > Apply from ADB**.
6. Sideload the ROM:

```bash
adb sideload your_rom.zip
```

7. Once the installation exits with no errors, restart recovery when prompted.

---

## Step 8 — Factory Reset and First Boot

1. In recovery, navigate to **Factory Reset** and confirm the action.
2. Reboot the device. The first boot may take several minutes.
3. Future ROM updates can be applied via the built-in Updater app or by rebooting into recovery and flashing a new ZIP.

> Updates are generally released monthly or quarterly, though this is best-effort and not guaranteed. ROMs with low adoption may eventually be discontinued.

---

## Step 9 (Optional) — Install Magisk and a Custom Recovery

> **Note:** If you intend to install a custom recovery, disable your screen lock and set the unlock method to **Swipe** beforehand. Some recoveries cannot decrypt the data partition if a PIN, password, or pattern lock is active before installation.

1. Download TWRP, OrangeFox, or PitchBlack Recovery from your device's page.
2. Boot into recovery mode.
3. To install a custom recovery, enable sideload and sideload it:

```bash
adb sideload your_custom_recovery.zip
```

4. Restart the device back into recovery mode to load the custom recovery.
5. In TWRP or PBRP, navigate to `/Magisk/Magisk.zip` and flash it directly. OrangeFox includes integrated Magisk support in its UI.
6. Reboot to the system and complete the Magisk setup in the app after checking it is up to date after configuring your internet connection.

> **Important:** Custom recoveries and Magisk are overwritten during ROM updates. Both must be reflashed after every update.

> ### Common Issues & Fixes
>
> **1. Black Screen After Booting Custom Recovery**
> - **Cause:** The ROM uses `vendor_boot`, which can cause issues with custom recoveries.
> - **Solution:**
>   1. Use a ROM that supports non-`vendor_boot` recoveries.
>   2. Ensure you flash both the `boot` and `vendor_boot` images correctly.
>
> **2. Screen Hangs in Custom Recovery**
> - **Cause:** The recovery may not support encryption, or the data partition may be corrupted.
> - **Action 1:** Disable your screen lock for better compatibility.
> - **Action 2:** If the issue persists, perform a factory reset or switch to a recovery that supports decryption.
>
> **3. Touchscreen/Sideloading Not Working in Recovery**
> - **Cause:** An outdated ROM or firmware may have loaded old vendor firmware.
> - **Solution:** Flash the latest ROM to update the vendor partition.
